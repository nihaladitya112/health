How the Program Works

A simple 4-step cycle to keep elderly patients healthy

1
Registration by Health Worker

A health worker (HW) visits the elderly person's home, collects their details, documents their disease, and sets up a medicine schedule and checkup plan in the system.

2
Daily Medicine Tracking

The patient or HW marks whether medicine was taken each day. If missed for 2+ consecutive days, the system auto-alerts the health worker to follow up.

3
Scheduled Checkup Reminders

Checkup dates are scheduled weekly, monthly, or quarterly. When a checkup is nearing, HWs get automatic reminders. Overdue checkups generate urgent alerts.

4
Emergency SOS on Demand

Family members or the patient can tap the SOS button anytime. The request is instantly logged and the HW, as well as the admin team, are notified for immediate action.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible for this program?
All elderly citizens (generally 60+ years) in Telangana are eligible, with priority given to those with chronic diseases like diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, or kidney problems.
How does a patient get registered?
A Health Worker assigned to your area will visit your home, collect details, and register the patient in the system. They will also provide a login username and password for you or your family to track progress.
What happens if medicine is missed?
If medicine is missed for 2 or more consecutive days, the system automatically sends an alert to the assigned Health Worker. The HW will follow up with the patient or family, and can mark medicines from their dashboard.
Is this service free?
Yes. This is a Telangana State Government initiative provided completely free of charge to all eligible citizens. There are no fees for registration, tracking, or any associated government healthcare services.
How do I use the SOS emergency button?
Go to the Emergency page and tap the SOS button. Fill in your name, age, phone number, and location. For life-threatening emergencies, always call 108 (Ambulance) first, then use the SOS form for system logging.

Need Help Enrolling an Elderly Patient?

Contact your nearest Government Hospital or call the Senior Citizen helpline. For emergencies, always call 108.
